Central Nervous System Infections

Overview

Antimicrobial dosing is based on a patient with normal renal function. For dosing based on impaired renal function, see Dosing of Antimicrobial Agents.

Bacterial meningitis is a MEDICAL EMERGENCY. Infectious disease consultation is STRONGLY RECOMMENDED.

Empiric antibiotic therapy should be started immediately if the diagnosis of bacterial meningitis is suspected and should not be delayed pending the results of head CT or diagnostic lumbar puncture (LP). If LP must be delayed, obtain blood cultures and start antibiotic therapy pending LP.

To achieve therapeutic levels in the cerebrospinal fluid (CSF), higher doses and more frequent dosing of antibiotics are typically required.

Herpes viruses (HSV, VZV) are the predominant cause of treatable encephalitis.

CSF should be sent for HSV and VZV PCR if encephalitis is suspected.

Have a low threshold to treat if encephalitis is suspected, as untreated mortality exceeds 70%.

Treatment: Acyclovir 10 mg/kg IV q8h for 14-21 days. Dosing should be based on ideal body weight (IBW). For dosing based on impaired renal function, see Dosing of Antimicrobial Agents.
